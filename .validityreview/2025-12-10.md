## Agenda
PRAGMA General Assembly (GA) Additional Meeting 


## 1. Notification of Sebastian Bode's resignation from the Administrative Board
The GA was formally notified of the resignation of Sebastian Bode from the Administrative Board. 

The resignattion was notified by the Chair and no objections or procedural concerns were raised. 

## 2. Election of replacement Member of the Board
Following the notification of Mr Sebastian Bode’s resignation, the General Assembly proceeded with the appointment of a replacement member of the Administrative Board in accordance with PRAGMA’s statutes.

The Board nominated Mr Benjamin Büergi to fill the vacant Board seat, and the General Assembly approved the appointment. Mr Büergi will serve for the remainder of Mr Bode’s original term, which expires in 2028 at the conclusion of the four-year term, at which point the seat will be subject to election.


## 3. Review of Q3 Board Report
The Q3 Board report was presented to the General Assembly.

Key topics highlighted during the review included:
- Legal and trademark matters
- Operational oversight activities
- Governance framework development
- Community and ecosystem engagement
- Progress on the Project Committee framework

The General Assembly acknowledged the report and no objections were raised.

## 4. Varia 

### 4.1 Open Coding Sessions: Structure and Expectations 
The GA discussed the structure and fascilitation of recent Open coding sessions. It was agreed tha the effctiveness of the Open coding sessions depends primarily on clear framing and stated intent and two formats of Open coding sessions were identified. 
- Discussion-oriented Open coding sessions that focus more on explanation, alignment and shared understanding
- Implementation-oriented Open coding sessions that focused on execution, code review and concrete technical outcomes

The GA agreed that clearly communicating the intended format of the Open coding sessions in advance would help set expectations and improve participation and effectiveness. 

### 4.2 Legal and Trademark Updates 
The GA received an update on legal and trademark matters including: 
- Decision not to engage trademark watching services at this time
- The ongoing PRAGMA trademark dispute and expected updates in January
- Legal cost support provided by the Cardano Foundation

### 4.3 Project Committee Framework 
An update was provided on the Project Committee: 
- 12 applications were received
- A target of three to give members was confirmed
- Applications are currently under review by the Board 


### 4.4 Mentorship
The GA discussed initiatives and achnowledged current capacity constraints. 

It was confirmed that the Open Coding Sessions currently serve as the initial mentoship mechanism, with potential future expansion into a more formal mentorship framework when resources allow. 


### 4.5 Website, CMS and Transparancy
The GA discussed improving transparency and activity visibility through:
- Increased website publishing
- Better utilization of the existing CMS
- CMS onboarding for non-technical contributors
- Development of content guidelines to reduce publishing friction
- Exploring automated surfacing of development activity from GitHub
