## Agenda
Additional General Assembly (GA) Meeting 

### 1. Notification of Sebastian Bode’s Resignation from the Administrative Board

The General Assembly was formally notified of the resignation of Sebastian Bode from the Administrative Board.
The resignation was acknowledged and no objections or procedural concerns were raised.

---

### 2. Election and Appointment of Replacement Member of the Administrative Board

Following the notification of Sebastian Bode’s resignation, the General Assembly proceeded with the appointment of a replacement member of the Administrative Board in accordance with PRAGMA’s statutes.
The Board nominated Benjamin Büergi to fill the vacant Board seat, and the General Assembly approved the appointment. Büergi will serve for the remainder of Mr Bode’s original term, which expires in 2028 at the conclusion of the four-year term, at which point the seat will be subject to election.

---

### 3. Review of Q3 Board Report

The Q3 Board report was presented to the General Assembly.

Key topics highlighted during the review included:
- Legal and trademark matters
- Operational oversight activities
- Governance framework development
- Community and ecosystem engagement
- Progress on the Project Committee framework

The General Assembly acknowledged the report and no objections were raised.

---

### 4. Varia

#### 4.1 Live Coding Session Structure and Expectations

The GA discussed the structure and facilitation of recent live coding sessions and reflected and t was agreed that the effectiveness of live coding sessions depends primarily on clear framing and stated intent. 

Two formats of live coding sessions were identified:
- Discussion-oriented live coding sessions, focused on explanation, alignment, and shared understanding
- Implementation-oriented live coding sessions, focused on execution, code review, and concrete technical outcomes

The GA agreed that clearly communicating the intended format of live coding sessions in advance would help set expectations and improve participation and effectiveness.


---

#### 4.2 Legal and Trademark Updates

The GA received an update on legal and trademark matters, including:
- The decision not to engage trademark watching services at this time
- The ongoing PRAGMA trademark dispute and expected updates in January
- Legal cost support provided by the Cardano Foundation

---

#### 4.3 Project Committee Framework

An update was provided on the Project Committee framework:
- Twelve applications were received
- A target of three to five members was confirmed
- Applications are currently under review by the Board

---

#### 4.4 Mentorship and Open Coding Sessions

The GA discussed mentorship initiatives and acknowledged current capacity constraints.
It was confirmed that Live coding sessions serve as the initial mentorship mechanism, with potential future expansion when resources allow.

---

#### 4.5 Website, CMS, and Transparency

The GA discussed improving transparency and activity visibility through:
- Increased website publishing
- Better utilization of the existing CMS
- CMS onboarding for non-technical contributors
- Development of content guidelines to reduce publishing friction
- Exploring automated surfacing of development activity from GitHub

---

### 6. Decisions and Resolutions
